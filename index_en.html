FEATURED MISSION

ATENEA on Artemis II

The Argentine microsatellite selected for NASA's crewed lunar mission.

WHAT IS ARTEMIS II?

Artemis II is the first crewed test flight of NASA's Artemis program, planned as a crewed lunar flyby mission. Alongside the primary mission, NASA is flying a set of international CubeSats as secondary payloads.

WHAT IS ATENEA?

ATENEA is a 12U Argentine CubeSat selected to fly as an international payload on Artemis II. Its objectives include radiation environment data, technology validation, and long-range communications demonstrations.

KEY CHALLENGES

Integration and safety standards tied to crewed mission context

Engineering documentation, validation, and review cycles

Power and battery-related safety requirements

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Is ATENEA going to the Moon?

ATENEA deploys as a CubeSat payload before the lunar flyby phase and operates in a high Earth orbit / deep space relevant environment.

Why does a CubeSat matter in a crewed mission?

Crewed missions demand strict safety and integration discipline. Meeting those requirements is a strong test of engineering maturity.
